## Executive Summary — Key Improvements at a Glance

### Biggest changes from the current site

- **Single hero, locked pricing.** The live site currently shows three different prices ($299, $499, $599) within one page — a major trust killer. The new spec locks $299 / $499 throughout.
- **Reviews moved up.** 140+ five-star reviews are the single strongest conversion asset on the site and currently sit halfway down the page. New layout features a trust strip immediately under the hero plus a full review wall before the final CTA.
- **Founder spotlight section.** Lucrece's name, face, and credentials get a dedicated authority block — the biggest E-E-A-T move on the page and the strongest signal both Google and AI search engines look for.
- **Honest "do you qualify" gate.** Mirrors the Neb. Rev. Stat. § 43-101 eligibility framework. Routes contested cases to Bundy Law LLC. UPL shield and conversion-quality booster in one move.
- **Traditional attorney vs. Adoption Forms Express comparison table** ($3K–$10K vs. $299–$499). AI summarizers like Perplexity and Google AI Overviews extract these tables almost verbatim.
- **Expanded FAQ with answer-first structure.** Every Q is an H3, every answer leads with the direct response — the structure that wins featured snippets and AI Overview citations.

### Keyword strategy is layered

Primary terms target Nebraska + stepparent adoption + forms in every variant. Secondary terms hit the cost/process/requirement queries. Long-tail captures the "can I do this without a lawyer" 11-PM searches. County-level keywords (Douglas, Lancaster, Sarpy, plus seven more) seed local SEO authority on the homepage and prep for dedicated county landing pages later.

### AI search optimization is built in throughout

Question-format headings, statute citations by name (§ 43-101 et seq., § 43-104), explicit numerical answers (cost ranges, timelines, age requirements), structured comparison tables, and full schema markup blocks (LegalService, AggregateRating, FAQPage, Person) — all the signals AI search engines weight heavily.

### 5.12 FAQ (Expanded — for Featured Snippets and AI Summarizers)

**H2:**
Frequently Asked Questions About Nebraska Stepparent Adoption

Each question is an H3 with a direct answer in the first sentence. This structure is what wins featured snippets and AI Overview citations.

**Q: How much does a stepparent adoption cost in Nebraska?**
With a traditional attorney, a Nebraska stepparent adoption typically costs $3,000 to $10,000. With Adoption Forms Express, attorney-prepared forms start at $299, plus a county filing fee of roughly $60–$90 paid directly to the court.

**Q: How long does a stepparent adoption take in Nebraska?**
Most uncontested Nebraska stepparent adoptions finalize in 4 to 6 months from the date you file your petition with the county court, though timelines vary by county and case complexity.

**Q: Can I do a stepparent adoption in Nebraska without a lawyer?**
Yes. Nebraska law does not require you to retain an attorney to file or finalize a stepparent adoption. Many uncontested cases are completed pro se using attorney-prepared forms like the ones available through Adoption Forms Express.

**Q: What forms do I need for a Nebraska stepparent adoption?**
A Nebraska stepparent adoption typically requires a Petition for Adoption, parental consent forms, a Notice of Adoption, a proposed Decree of Adoption, and supporting documents such as background check authorizations and proof of marriage. Adoption Forms Express provides all required forms compliant with Neb. Rev. Stat. § 43-101 et seq.

**Q: What if the other biological parent won't consent?**
If the absent biological parent has abandoned the child for at least 6 months prior to filing, Nebraska courts may waive the consent requirement under Neb. Rev. Stat. § 43-104. Abandonment must be proven to the court, and contested cases are generally outside the scope of DIY forms — we recommend consulting an adoption attorney such as Bundy Law LLC.

**Q: What are the requirements to be a stepparent adopter in Nebraska?**
You must be at least 19 years old, at least 10 years older than the child, legally married to the child's biological parent, and the child must reside in your home for at least 6 months before finalization.

**Q: Are these forms accepted in Nebraska county courts?**
Yes. The forms are drafted by a licensed Nebraska adoption attorney, comply with Neb. Rev. Stat. § 43-101 et seq., and are kept current with Nebraska adoption law and local court practice.

**Q: What counties in Nebraska do you serve?**
All 93 Nebraska counties — including Douglas (Omaha), Lancaster (Lincoln), Sarpy, Hall (Grand Island), Buffalo (Kearney), Scotts Bluff, Madison (Norfolk), Platte (Columbus), Dodge (Fremont), and Adams (Hastings) counties.

**Q: What if my case is contested?**
DIY forms are designed for uncontested cases only. If your case is contested — or if you are unsure — we recommend a consultation with Bundy Law LLC, our affiliated full-service Nebraska adoption practice.

**Q: Do I still need an attorney for the court hearing?**
No. Most uncontested Nebraska stepparent adoption hearings are short and procedural. Your forms package includes a court hearing prep guide with the questions a judge is most likely to ask.
